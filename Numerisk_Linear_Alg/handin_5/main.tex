\subsection*{(f) - Describing Geometry with SVD}
SVD is essentialy the factorization of a matrix where each variable of the SVD describes a part of the matrix, more specifically $u$(left singular vectors) and $s$(singular values) describes the geometry while $vt$ is the right singular vectors.\\
Even more specifically $u$ is a $2\times2$ matrix on the form $[u_0 | u_1]$ where the column vector $u_0$ describes the direction on where the variance is the largest and $u_1$  describes the direction where the variance is the smallest, note since $u$ is orthogonal the lenght of both $u_0$ and $u_1$ is 1.\\
This is where $s$ comes in! Whereas $u$ describes the direction of thte variance, the singular points in $s$ describes the magnitude of the variance.\\
So the singular values describe how much the figure 'stretches' while the left singular vectors is the direction of the stretch.
